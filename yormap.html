YorMap is an interactive way-finding system designed for students of the University of York.

In this quick 4-week project, we were tasked as a team to design a means for students of the university of York to get to their desired lessons on time and route them specifically to the classrooms they need to arrive at.

We were specifically asked to keep these designes low-fidelity, with a focus on solutions rather than aesthetics.

Identifying User Needs and Capabilities.

We conducted 15 interviews and performed an affinity diagram categorising the core issues that students have while trying to attain their goals and fulfil their needs on campus.

Persona's.

We transformed the identified needs into user 2 persona's that holisitcally captured our primary user groups. I designed these using Adobe XD.

Scenario's.

In order to contextualise the usage of the applications, we performed As-is and To-be scenarios. Said method is effectively assuming the role of the user in a narrative and envisioning their happy path from both before our app is launched using current solutions (As-is), and then afterwards with our solutions put in place (To-be).

These are discussed in detail in the full report, however the end result was a storyboard of our 'To-be' scenario.

Wireframes.

We then got to low-fidelity wireframes and prototypes to get a feel for how the user will move through the interface. We designed for the following functionalities:

  • Search for a Room or Location on Campus.
  • Specify Route via Criteria (Wheelchair accessibility).
  • Directions via a Defined Route.
  • Emergency and Saftey Features.

    After some expert review and user testing, we had refined the interface into something fairly usable. The full report goes into some (excrutiating) detail about this process but I hope this provides some idea of the project.

While it's not the most aesthetically pleasing design, it is representative of the most complex system I have designed to date.
